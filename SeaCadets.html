The USNSCC

The United States Naval Sea Cadet Corps, as well as the League Cadet Corps are youth leadership programs for people ages 11-18 run by the Navy League of the United States. Its goal is to develop young men and women into excellent future leaders. USNSCC Units have a rank structure, wear and care for uniforms, as well as perform a multitude of other activites to train cadets. Members of the USNSCC are not members of the US Military.

The John T. Dempster Division

Cadets join Units located all across the country, I am part of the John T. Dempster Division. Visit the division's website to learn more.

Trainings

Over the summer and the winter, Sea Cadets go on trainings at many locations. These trainings can range from recruit trainings, to advanced trainings that teach topics like aviation, sailing, seamanship, marksmanship, and other amazing experiences. Below is list of trainings I have attended

  • NLCC SEAL Training, Camp Pendleton, CA
  • NSCC Recruit Training, RTC Great Lakes
  • Master At Arms Training, Kings Bay, Georgia
  • Sailing, Advanced, SSV Oliver Hazard Perry
  • Petty Officer Leadership Academy, Ft. Dix, NJ
  • NSCC Recruit Training (Staff), Ft. Devens, MA

Inspection

Each year, every unit goes through a process called annual inspection, where the unit is tested on every aspect to judge how well the division does in comparison to to others. The Dempster Division takes pride in their performace during annual inspections, and we've placed in the top 10 divisions in the country multiple times.

Color Guard

The John T. Dempster Division participates in color guard events often. I'm proud to have been part of their color guard team, and to have participated in so many events.
