Technology Student Association

Recently, I've joined my high school's Technology Student Association club. It's an organization that hosts a yearly contest in each state about a multitude of STEM concepts, from mechanical engineering to cybersecurity. During the 2019-2020 school year I aimed to participate in Board Game Design, Video Game Development, and Cybersecurity activities, but unfortunately only the Cybersecurity activity could be held virtually due to COVID-19

Video Game Development Club

I have a passion for video games in general, and developing them has been an undertaking of mine for a while. I'm a member of the school's Video Game Development Club, where students come together and group up to develop video games, primarily using the Unity Engine available on the school's computers.

Unity

Aside from the club, I've endeavored in developing video games using the Unity engine and the C# scripting language since middle school. I've made a 2D platformer, a Galaga-style arcade game, and more recently a physics-based maze game with randomly generated maps. Alongside the Unity Engine I use Pixel art programs to create artwork and animations for the games.

Computer Programming

I am very much a computer person, from my passion with video games to my understanding of software and programming. I first started to teach myself Java at the site Codeacademy.com, and gradually started to expand out to other things, from Unity's C# to HTML and CSS, and of course Java in Android Studio. A lot of my skills were self-taught before I started taking computer science classes in high school, and I continue to work and improve my ability to code.
